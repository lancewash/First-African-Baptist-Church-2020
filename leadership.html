Reverend Beverly S. Hudson

Reverend Beverly S. Hudson accepted Jesus Christ and was baptized August 1975 at Mount Hermon Missionary Baptist Church in Bishopville, South Carolina under the leadership of the late Rev. Bert D. Lewis Sr This is where she gained her biblical foundation and passion to serve the Lord.

Rev. Bev graduated in 1986 from Morris College in Sumter, South Carolina with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Mathematics. Rev. Bev is also a 2007 graduate of Jameson School of Ministry with a Certificate in Biblical Studies and she is currently matriculating in the Master of Divinity Program at Palmer Theological Seminary. Professionally, she is an employee with the Department of Treasury.

Rev. Bev is a member of the historic First African Baptist Church in Darby Township, Pennsylvania where the Reverend Dr. Richard A. Dent is the Pastor. In addition to her Ministerial Duties, Rev. Bev also serves in the following ministries: New Members Discipleship Training – Adult and Youth Instructor, the Word of Life Bookstore, Women’s Ministry, Youth Ministry and the Women of Faith Support Ministry.

When she was lost, Rev. Bev sought the Lord, He heard her and delivered her from all of her fears; at this point, she rededicated her life back to Jesus Christ and has not looked back. As she began to study and meditate on the Word of God, she found herself experiencing the deeper things of God. After much prayer and fasting, she began to seek God for His will for her life and the result was her call to preach the Good News of Jesus Christ; she was licensed to preach on June 26, 2011 and ordained on November 4, 2012 by Rev. Dr. Richard A. Dent.

The Lord blessed Rev. Bev with one son, Chad Jonathan Hudson. For such a time as this, she stands on God’s promises and relies on, “Being confident of this, that He who began a good work in us will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus” – Philippians 1:6.

Reverend Horace Kinlaw

Reverend Horace Kinlaw is an Associate Minister of the historic First African Baptist Church in Darby Township, Pennsylvania where the Reverend Dr. Richard A Dent is the Pastor. In addition to his ministerial duties Reverend Kinlaw also serves in the following ministries: Sunday school, New Members Discipleship Class, Neighbor to Neighbor Community Development Corporation, Bible Study and the Youth Ministry.

Reverend Horace Kinlaw attended New Life Bible Institute Missionary Training School in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.  He received his license to preach in 2012 at Hopewell Baptist Church. He was ordained to preach in 2015 after completing ordination training from The Bethany Baptist Association School of Biblical Studies. 

In his walk with Christ and due to his continued faithfulness, God appointed him with the responsibility of providing the spiritual oversight of the children and congregation at Ranch Hope, LeeAnn Bailey Memorial Chapel as their Pastor and Youth Pastor of Hopewell Baptist Church. Reverend Kinlaw loves the local church, and enjoys seeing people connect with God as well as in authentic relationships and community. His leadership and administrative gifts, love for preaching and teaching, and his heart for seeing people grow are his primary strengths in ministry.  He has a great passion for young people and has a way of connecting with them to strive for the best. Reverend Kinlaw is also the founder of the Alpha J Omega Youth Organization for young men between the ages of 12-18, which is a mentoring program.

 Reverend Kinlaw is married to Keisha and has two children: Raymond and Sanaiya. In his free time, he enjoys sports of all types and fellowships with those in the community. He is currently pursuing his Master’s Degree from Trinity Bible College and Seminary.

Minister Sharon White

Minister White is an associate minister of The First African Baptist Church of Darby Township, PA serving under the leadership of Reverend Dr. Richard A. Dent. She is the Director of Sunday School and Children's Church. Prior to her prayerful submission to God’s Divine move to First African, Minister White served faithfully for 27 years at Sharon Baptist Church of Philadelphia, PA, under the leadership of Bishop Keith W. Reed, Sr.

While at Sharon Baptist Minister White served in ministry in the following capacities:

  • Director of “Empowerment Hour” (Sunday School) for which she was responsible for the full operation including strategic planning, curriculum design, the hiring, training, and the skill development of all new instructors. She also developed a screening and evaluative process for new instructors and created training programs for all instructors.
  • Instructor in the Sharon Baptist Bible Institute (SBBI), for which she taught the following courses: “Introduction to the Bible”, “How to Study the Bible”, Bad Girls of the Bible” and “The Names of God”.
  • Staff member & instructor for the Women of Grace Women’s Ministry, which also includes teaching courses such as “Developing Your Personal Relationship with The Lord Through His Word”, a 4 part series on intimacy with God and “Single to Serve”.
  • Administrative support to the Pastors for the Associate Ministers’ Ministry.

Minister White received her certification as a Biblical Counselor through The Christian Research and Development Institute in Philadelphia. She also received certificates of completion for Evangelism through Coral Ridge Evangelism Explosion Program as well as The Bible Training for Church Leaders from the Bible Training Center for Pastors (2 1/2 year training program) at Aldan Union Church on January 18, 2015.

Professionally, Minister White is a licensed clinical social worker and is the Program Director for geriatric mobile services at NHS Human Services of Delaware County. She holds a BSW from Eastern College and a Masters of Social Service degree from Bryn Mawr College Graduate School of Social Work & Social Research. She serves on numerous professional boards in the aging & mental health field and has conducted numerous workshops and presentations on a variety of topics including cultural diversity, mental health, depression, aging, etc. Minister White is past adjunct professor in the social work department at Eastern University.

Her passion is preaching and teaching the Word of God and ministering to His people!

Life verse is Lamentations 3:22 & 23, understanding very well that God’s mercies and faithfulness are unmatched!

Minister Kenneth D. Wilson

Brother Kenneth J. D. Wilson was born a twin to the late Patricia Wilson and Kent Wilson in Nuremberg, Germany on November 8, 1980.

Kenneth and his twin brother Kevin were raised by their grandparents, George (deceased) and Mary Atkings who took them to church and introduced them to the music ministry at an early age. Kenneth was baptized and spiritually cultivated at Second Pilgrim Baptist Church where he served on the youth choir and usher board. He later became a member of the New Way Missionary Baptist Church where he was an active youth leader, Junior Deacon, Usher and Choir Director.

After graduating from Saint Joseph’s Preparatory School in 1999, Kenneth went on to earn his Bachelor of Arts degree in Sociology from the University of Connecticut and subsequently earned a Master’s in Criminal Justice with a concentration in Behavioral Analysis in 2006. Currently, he is matriculating in the Master of Divinity Program at Palmer Theological Seminary.

Professionally, Kenneth is the Assistant Director of the Eva Donaldson School of Music. Driven by the need to mentor young men of all ages, Kenneth is the founder of “A Different Breed of Men”, a non-profit organization.

After many years of wrestling with what he knew to be the voice of the Lord, Kenneth accepted the call of ministry on his life in March 2007.

As a faithful member of First African Baptist Church, Kenneth continues to prune his gift for ministry as he actively serves in the following ministries: Discipleship training, Marriage Ministry, Men’s Ministry, Chosen 300 and Music Ministry.

On May 12, 2012, Kenneth was united in marriage to his long time love Nicole G. Bynum; the Lord blessed them with three children: Tahjae, JaDen and Kai.

In his servitude to the Lord, one of Kenneth’s favorite scripture is, “Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: them he also glorified. What shall we then say to these things? If God be for us who can be against us?” - Romans 8:30-31
